2.What we collect

When you take the LDNA assessment, we collect:

  • Identification data: your full name and email address.
  • Professional context: your current role/title and years of experience.
  • Assessment responses: the behavioral words you select in Parts 1 and 2 of the questionnaire, and the counts of those selections.
  • Consent record: the date and time you granted consent, the version of this policy in effect at that moment, and your opt-in choices for follow-up communications.
  • Technical metadata: the date and time of submission.

We do not collect: special categories of personal data (such as health, religion, ethnicity, political views), payment information, government identifiers, geolocation, device identifiers beyond what is inherent in any web request, or behavioral tracking from outside the assessment page.

6.How long we keep it

  • Assessment responses and profile data: kept for as long as you have an active relationship with us, and for up to 24 months after your most recent interaction, after which the personally identifying fields are deleted or anonymized.
  • Consent records: kept for 6 years after the consent is given or withdrawn, to comply with our legal obligation to demonstrate the basis of processing.
  • Marketing opt-in flags: kept until you opt out or until we cease the related communications, whichever is sooner.

We will not keep data longer than necessary for the purposes for which it was collected.

7.Your rights

Depending on where you live, you have the following rights regarding your personal data. We will respond to verified requests within the timeframes required by applicable law (typically 30 days under GDPR; 45 days under CCPA/CPRA).

  • Right to know / access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you and information about how we process it.
  • Right to rectification — correct any data that is inaccurate or incomplete.
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") — ask us to delete your personal data, subject to legal retention requirements.
  • Right to restriction of processing — ask us to stop using your data while a dispute is resolved.
  • Right to data portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object — object to processing based on legitimate interests, including profiling for marketing purposes.
  • Right to withdraw consent — withdraw any consent you've given, at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out before the withdrawal.
  • California-specific rights — California residents additionally have the right to opt out of any "sale" or "sharing" of personal information (we do neither), the right to limit the use of sensitive personal information, and the right to non-discrimination for exercising any of these rights.

To exercise any of these rights, email info@thesauntergroup.com from the email address you used to take the assessment. We may ask for additional information to verify your identity.

8.Automated decision-making and profiling

The LDNA assessment uses your responses to generate a behavioral profile that is interpreted by us and shared with you. While the underlying profile uses an automated framework, all profile interpretation involves human review. We do not use the assessment to make decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects on you without human involvement.

If you believe an interpretation of your profile is incorrect, you can contact us to request review.

9.Children's data

The LDNA assessment is intended for adults. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under the age of 16. If you believe a minor has submitted data to us, contact us and we will delete it.

10.Security

We use industry-standard administrative and technical safeguards to protect your data, including encryption in transit (HTTPS) and at rest, multi-factor authentication on all administrative accounts that can access your data, role-based access controls on the underlying storage environment, and access limited to authorized personnel on a need-to-know basis. The platforms we rely on operate under widely recognized independent security assurance (SOC 2 / ISO 27001-type frameworks). No method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure, but we work to maintain a level of protection appropriate to the sensitivity of the data.

11.Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the Effective date and Version at the top. For material changes, we will make a reasonable effort to notify you (for example, by email if we hold your address) and, where required, seek fresh consent. The version of the policy in effect when you consented is recorded against your assessment record.
