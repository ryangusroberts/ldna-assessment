Why companies stall

Growth doesn't forgive structural debt. It amplifies it.

At $300K–$1M/month, the architecture that got you here starts breaking. We see the same six patterns repeat.

The founder is the bottleneck

Every major decision still routes through one person. Velocity drops the day the founder gets sick.

Decisions go reactive

The team puts out fires instead of building strategically. Planning becomes impossible.

Visibility breaks down

You don't see what's failing until it's already broken. Dashboards lie or don't exist.

Leadership outgrows structure

You've grown as a leader, but the org chart, decision rights, and accountability haven't kept pace.

The team is mis-paired

The right people are in the wrong seats, or paired with people who share their blind spots, not cover them.

Systems were never built for scale

What worked at $50K/month breaks at $200K. Your operating rhythm was never designed for the current load.
